How I Got Into Programming

In my freshman year of highschool, I ran out of electives and took intro to programming. I had always loved technology, but didn't have any thoughts on programming. That class taught me two things... the first, that I loved programming. The second, that block programming like Scratch was a little too limited.
I convinced my guidance counselor to put me a year ahead into Visual Basic, which I spent the whole year using to make games on my graphing calculator. Then I took AP Computer Science and learned most of the core ideas of OOP and, more importantly, how to learn technologies on my own.

Tetris in TI-BASIC

The first real, large project I had with coding was more of an extra-curricular. I had become so enamored with coding that I searched for ways to do it while in my other classes. My programming teacher had told the class about the kinds of things he had coded in his calculator in high school.

At first I just built programs to automate annoying formulas or stuff that would make my friends laugh. This quickly turned into me wanting to make something huge, and I set my eyes on tetris (in the day, it was default on pretty much every phone, and I liked the demo a lot).

If you've never used a Basic language, let me save you the trouble: it's garbage. An non-object oriented language is bad enough, but one where composition is also nearly impossible means it's about as easy as coding in Vim on a key with half the keys missing and a different layout. Despite that, I had made a successful model of tetris with everything short of collision detection.....

Then the batteries fell out.



Always back up your code.

Server DevOps & Hosting

A short example of me deploying and using an SSH server for an application (a modded Minecraft server)

My friends and I have a twice-annual pasttime of playing a game called Tekkit together. Originally, since I had the strongest computer, it made sense for me to host. Non-dedicated servers have come a LONG way since then, but P2P tunneling with Hamachi made it manageable. Deploying and redeploying and reteaching everyone to install become a hastle, so around senior year I started renting server spaces on DigitalOcean and using a VM of Ubuntu to do the hosting for me, and aggragate the mod build at a static URL. If nothing else, my patience for coaching 5+ people to manually update their clients earned this a spot on my portfolio.
