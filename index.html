Who I Am

I'm a 24 year old man living in the greater Richmond area. I'm a nerd, a technophile, a weightlifter, and pretty much anything else I can cram into the 24-hour day.

How I Got Into Programming

In my freshman year of highschool, I ran out of electives and took intro to programming. I had always loved technology, but didn't have any thoughts on programming. That class taught me two things... the first, that I loved programming. The second, that block programming like Scratch was a little too limited.
I convinced my guidance counselor to put me a year ahead into Visual Basic, which I spent the whole year using to make games on my graphing calculator. Then I took AP Computer Science and learned most of the core ideas of OOP and, more importantly, how to learn technologies on my own.

Tetris in TI-BASIC

Once upon a time, in sophomore year, I programmed Tetris in my graphic calculator in classes where the teacher couldn't see exactly what I was doing. I had made it over halfway through programming before the nature of volatile memory ripped it from me and taught me for the first time how important backups are. I've been tempted to recreate this to say I've finished it, but not even Phase-0 of quarantine was enough boredom to pick up such an uncooperative language again.

PC Building (and Fixing)

My personal favorite of my builds

When I first moved to Virginia, I forewent furniture in exchange for PC parts (I still don't regret it) and built my first PC. Since then, I believe I've built six or seven of all shapes and sizes for anyone that asks. Unfortunately, whenever you become the family member that "built his own computer," you become the family IT man indefinitely. So I've had my fair share of software and hardware troubleshooting.

Server Hosting

My friends and I have a twice-annual pasttime of playing a game called Tekkit together. Originally, since I had the strongest computer, it made sense for me to host. Non-dedicated servers have come a LONG way since then, but P2P tunneling with Hamachi made it manageable. Deploying and redeploying and reteaching everyone to install become a hastle, so around senior year I started renting server spaces on DigitalOcean and using a VM of Ubuntu to do the hosting for me, and aggragate the mod build at a static URL. If nothing else, my patience for coaching 5+ people to update their clients manually earned this a spot on my portfolio.

3D Modeling & Manufacturing

Python

I've used lots of languages and continue to learn and explore more, but Python will always be my favorite. It may not be the most efficient or customizable language, but I think it embodies the spirit of coders in general. The PIP tool makes automating daily tasks a downhill trot instead of an uphill battle, and truly Pythonic code can speak for itself in a way CPP or JS will never be able to.
